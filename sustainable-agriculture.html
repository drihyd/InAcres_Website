Sustainable Agriculture

The post-independence era of India brought with it the challenge of sustaining a growing population. To help ease the situation, the farming community acted reactively to resolve the situation by cultivating high-yield crop varieties with an infusion of irrigated water, fertilizers and pesticides.

While such a quick resolution created a food surplus, it also caused a plethora of concerns like soil degradation, environmental pollution, pesticide toxicity and a huge blow to the sustainability of the agricultural practices. Soon, it became evident that conventional farming had to be relooked at. Particularly, concerns were raised because of an alarming increase in the use of synthetic pesticides and fertilizers.

Soon, the farming communities decided to go back to the roots of safe, organic and eco-friendly practices of agriculture. With constant awareness and research, today, organic farming has been proven as a sustainably viable alternative. It is known to produce more yield when compared to the monoculture cropping method.

However, organic farming does require quite a lot of know-how before you begin implementing it. From the nature of the soil to the crop variety, it is crucial that you have the necessary clarity.

Being a holistic production and management system, sustainable organic farming promotes and enhances the health of the ecosystem and everything that thrives in it. This includes the natural regulation and promotion of biological activity in the soil, elimination of synthetic fertilizers and pesticides that gradually results in cleaner air, water and soil.

The ultimate goal of sustainable organic farming is to optimize the health and interdependence of different ecosystems living together.
