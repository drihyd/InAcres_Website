Our CSA Community

InAcres Shadnagar-CSA is in close proximity to Hyderabad, yet it is undisturbed by the urban way of life. It is a serene rural, rural and idyllic landscape in itself. Shadnagar-CSA farm is spread across a 100-acre virgin land located near Bheemaram village. Most of the development activities have been completed.

Project Highlights

  • Equipped with fertile soil that’s suitable for multi-cropping model [Short term: Vegetables, Mid Term: Multiple varieties of fruits & Long Term: Sandalwood]
  • Great availability of groundwater due to a stream that connects to the free-flowing Dindi river that is around 500 meters along the northern side of the CSA farm.
  • Home to an exclusive eco village to train passionate individuals in farming, with access to a farm stay and farm tourism.
  • Our flagship farm at Shadnagar is pleasant in all seasons and is a role-model with active farming and value-added infrastructure.

What makes our Shadnagar CSA farm special?

Rich living Soil

While selecting the plot in Shadnagar, the soil was one of the important factors to consider. It was a virgin land which hadn’t been introduced to chemical fertilisers or pesticides in the past. Also, the top layer (up to 6 inches) of the soil which is the most crucial aspect in farming was not disturbed by tilling or erosion.

The organisms living in the soil play a significant role in maintaining the soil’s health and the plants it nurtures. Such organisms also influence every aspect of decomposition and nutrient availability.

Keeping all of this in mind, we ensured a nutrient-rich ecosystem that was equipped sufficiently to nurture and foster the cultivation of an array of crops that can sustain different climatic conditions


Sustainable water source

Constant and regular availability of water also plays a vital role while selecting the farming module. Since sustainability and responsible farming practices are values of high regard at InAcres, we only utilise underground water as the main source of irrigation at our Shadnagar CSA farm.

Due to an increase in uncertainty in the rainfall patterns all across the state and India, we teamed up with the Department of Geophysics at Osmania University, Hyderabad to conduct an extensive geophysical survey. This helped us identify and define various mechanisms to preserve and recharge the levels of underground water including the landscapes with the least amount of seasonal rainfall.

Below are a few steps that we have followed to implement them -

3-feet deep trenches based on elevation & topography have been dug along the site’s periphery to prevent the rainwater from escaping into neighbouring lands.
At every 10 metres of the trench, contour bands have been created that connect to a recharge pit to help the groundwater recharge evenly across the entire site.
Excessive water, if any, is diverted to a separate percolation tank that maintains and regulates the levels of groundwater across the entire site
An open well that is designed to permit the incoming seepage from the percolation tank to keep up the water level in it, has also been dug.
Out of the suggested 25 bore well points, we have successfully installed 10 of them that are well within 200 feet in depth to keep the alkalinity levels low.
Two storage tanks of 70 litres each have been installed to avoid direct water feed to the drips from the borewells to maintain a TDS below 250.

Agroforest Model

We practise the new paradigm in agriculture - agroforestry, that integrates the cultivation of trees and crops on a vast farmscape to create a sustainable, nutrient-rich and eco-friendly environment. This engenders an organic farm of high soil fertility, nitrogen fixation while reducing the soil erosion and groundwater loss.

We grow short term, mid-term and long term crops that include vegetables & leafy greens; fruits and sandalwood respectively.

We follow Vrikshayurveda, written by Maharshi Surapala to cultivate organic produce that is rich in mineral and nutrient content. Intermingled with the subject of ‘arbori-horticulture’, the concept of Vrikshayurveda throws light on soil sustainability and how the food, shelter and aesthetic requirements of different strata of the society can be met.

Celebration of togetherness

Our great nation was built on the grounds of community and togetherness. However, rapid urbanisation has led to the growth of nuclear families all the while losing the essence of community living.

At InAcres, we bring together like-minded and passionate individuals to form a community and bring back the essence of togetherness. Because miracles can happen when a large group of like-minded individuals work towards a shared goal.

As a part of the community development, we conduct multiple activities at our farms like festival celebrations, activities for kids and the elderly, nature walks and farm stays. By becoming a part of our CSA farm, you not only have access to a sustainable organic farm but also play a key role in striking a socio-economic balance.
